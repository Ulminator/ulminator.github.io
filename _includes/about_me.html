About Me

I am a software engineer in the Catalog space of the Merchandising department at The Home Depot with a Professional Data Engineer Certification. So far during my time there I have been a part of three separate teams that have each had their own unique challenges dealing with product data either in Google Cloud or in our on premise data centers.

I have an inquisitive mind and am naturally drawn to taking on challenging problems. I obtained my Bachelor of Science Degree with a double major in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science from Emory University in the fall of 2017, where I received a full tuition scholarship. Previously I worked at SFL Scientific as a Data Science Consulting intern and The University of Cambridge as a VICHF-IREU fellow.

Before starting my job at Home Depot, I was unfamilliar with how software was built at scale, but as my knowledge of the software development lifecyle increased, my interests in operational work dealing with ensuring the scalability and reliability of distributed systems has grown. I have enjoyed designing, building, and operationalizing data processing systems and am open to data engineering positions that allow me to further develop those skills in addition to allowing me to deploy machine learning models to production.
